Definitions

Metric Description What It Tells You
Mean Radius (ATC) Average distance of all shots to the mathematical center of the group. The true statistical consistency of the group, heavily reducing the skew of a single flyer.
Extreme Spread (CTC) / Group Size Center-to-center distance between the two furthest shots in a group. The absolute worst-case dispersion of that specific string. Highly susceptible to outliers.
Circular Error Probable (CEP 90%) The radius of a circle, centered on the mean point of impact, expected to contain a specific percentage (usually 50% or 90%) of shots. Useful for predictive performance and rigorous statistical ranking.
MR Confidence (95%) The 95% confidence interval for the true population Mean Radius. Indicates the mathematical reliability of the observed Mean Radius based on sample size.
Vertical Spread / SD The vertical dispersion (Y-axis) of the shots. SD represents the average variance. Often used to diagnose variations in muzzle velocity or breathing control.
Horizontal Spread / SD The horizontal dispersion (X-axis) of the shots. SD represents the average variance. Often points to wind estimation errors or trigger control issues.
A-ZED
(A-Zone Effective Distance)		 The theoretical maximum distance where 95% of shots would land within an IPSC A-zone (15cm). Transforms abstract angular precision into a practical maximum effective range benchmark.
Dispersion Analysis Analyzes the mathematical relationship (R²) between vertical dispersion and velocity variations. Detects if vertical stringing is directly caused by velocity issues or by unrelated rifle tuning.
